Swoosh News is a community-oriented, social news website with content that hackers might find interesting. It is based on and modeled after Hacker News. Hacker News is an experiment with a goal to create a community site that will not decline in quality as it becomes popular. Their hypothesis is that by making a conscious effort to resist decline, they can keep it from happening.

I am a long time reader of Hacker News. My oldest account is from late 2007, but I started lurking a few months before that. For myself and many others, Hacker News has not met its goal of keeping its high standard. As it gets more popular, the signal-to-noise ratio continues to fall, and it is beginning to devolve into Proggit. The quality of comments has particularly gone down hill. In an effort to create a site that could become popular while still meeting this high standard, I created Swoosh News.

Swoosh News is based on Hacker News (even the layout!), but it has one main difference. At Swoosh News, it costs money to post comments and to vote on comments. The cost to post a comment and vote is included on the Price page. With this small barrier of entry, I hope to keep the quality of comments high, regardless of how popular the site gets.

What is 'high quality' with regard to comments? A high quality comment is one that is both concise and informative. It is well thought-out, it probably contains links to further information, it might contain an explanation of a confusing point in the original article, it might be a highly relevant personal experience. On the other hand, it does not contain any puns, it is not overly critical, it is not a one-word quip, and it is not a random personal experience. Look at the Guidelines for more information.

The goal of Swoosh News is to be like Hacker News on Erlang days.
